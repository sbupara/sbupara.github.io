About Me 

To explain the direction of my work, it helps to go back to the beginning. My interests in psychology and social consciousness since childhood led me to UC Berkeley, where I studied philosophy of mind and social science under the mentorship of renowned American philosophers John Searle and Hubert Dreyfus. After graduating with my undergraduate degree, Searle connected me with a quantum-physicist turned visual neuroscientist, Professor and Researcher at UC Berkeley, Stanley Klein. Taking me under his wing, Klein introduced me to the exciting world of academic research as a research assistant through lab manager (2007-2013). Along the way, I honed a strong prowess for the business component of research: bringing money in, while managing projects and the funds tied to them. During this same period, the expertise I developed building computers and writing code in the lab, combined with my interests in finance and social sciences, led me down the Bitcoin and cryptocurrency rabbit hole. My full-time career transitioned from the practice of research to the business of research, and I spent the next five years managing multi-million-dollar research portfolios in Academia. Currently, I am working on an MBA with a focus on the intersection between disruptive technologies and emerging markets in the new digital economy.
